Microplastics & Human Health

Understanding the health implications of microplastic exposure

Critical Health Concerns
Organ Accumulation
Research has identified microplastic particles in liver, kidney, and brain tissue samples.
Blood Contamination
Studies have detected plastic particles circulating in human bloodstream.
Placental Transfer
Microplastics can cross the placental barrier, affecting fetal development.
Respiratory & Digestive Impact
Lung Inflammation
Airborne microplastic particles cause chronic respiratory irritation.
Gut Microbiome
Ingested particles may alter beneficial bacteria populations.
Intestinal Barrier
Chronic exposure may weaken the intestinal lining integrity.
Cellular & Hormonal Effects
Endocrine Disruption
Chemical additives interfere with hormone production and function.
Oxidative Stress
Microplastic exposure accelerates cellular damage and aging.
Immune Response
Foreign particles trigger chronic inflammatory responses.
